A LITTLE ABOUT CHRISTOPHER JUDGE

Christopher E. Judge is a trial and appellate lawyer actively engaged in the private practice of law throughout the greater San Francisco Bay Area since 1972. During that time he has handled a wide variety of trials and appeals before both state and federal courts and administrative agencies that include criminal defense, white collar crimes, business, corporate and securities disputes, family law, torts, aviation law, juvenile law and state workers' compensation claims. He has acted as a judge pro tempore of the Contra Costa County Courts, is a member of the Contra Costa County and Los Angeles County Bar Associations, a licensed private pilot and member of AOPA, and an adjunct professor in aviation and business law with Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University at Travis Air Force Base in Fairfield, California.
Located in Richmond, California, for over 35 years, the law firm's current emphasis is in the areas of personal injury, workers' compensation, juvenile dependency, and related family law and consumer matters.

Services

General civil and criminal trial and appellate practice in California and Federal Courts, with current emphasis in personal injury and state workers' compensation litigation. Criminal law experience ranges from misdemeanors to serious non-capital felonies at both trial and appellate levels. Civil law experience spans virtually all areas of private litigation, including bankruptcy, contract, domestic relations, employment law, personal and industrial injury, social security, wrongful death, real property and securities fraud. Has personally tried numerous jury and non-jury cases to verdict or decision in both California state and federal district courts as well as various governmental administrative agencies, and argued appeals before the California Courts of Appeals and the United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit.
